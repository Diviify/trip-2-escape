A well-packed bag can make the difference between an exhausting slog and an exhilarating Himalayan adventure. Whether you're heading to Manali, Spiti, or the Kumaon hills, these principles will keep your load manageable.

## Start with a layering system

Mountain weather shifts quickly. Pack three versatile layers instead of bulky single-purpose items:

- **Base layer** — moisture-wicking merino or synthetic tops
- **Mid layer** — fleece or light down jacket
- **Shell** — waterproof, breathable jacket with taped seams

## Footwear matters most

Your boots are non-negotiable. Break them in at least three weeks before departure. Add:

- Two pairs of trekking socks (merino wool dries fast)
- Camp shoes or sandals for lodge evenings
- Blister kit with moleskin and antiseptic

## Smart weight-saving swaps

Leave the hardcover books at home. Download maps offline, carry a 10,000mAh power bank, and use dry bags instead of bulky rain covers for your entire pack.

### What to leave behind

Hair dryers, multiple jeans, and full-size toiletries add kilograms fast. Transfer liquids to travel-size containers and share sunscreen with your trekking partner.

## Altitude essentials

Above 3,000 metres, pack sunscreen (SPF 50+), lip balm, diamox if your doctor recommends it, and a reusable water bottle with purification tablets. Hydration is your best defence against altitude sickness.

## Final checklist

Weigh your pack before you fly. Aim for under 12 kg including water for multi-day treks. If it feels heavy in your living room, it will feel brutal on a switchback.
