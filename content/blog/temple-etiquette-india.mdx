India's temples are living spiritual spaces, not museums. Observing local customs shows respect and often grants you a warmer welcome from priests and devotees alike.

## Dress code basics

Cover shoulders and knees. Scarves are invaluable — wrap one around your waist if you're wearing shorts. Remove leather items (belts, wallets) where requested, as many traditions associate leather with death.

## Footwear and cleanliness

Always remove shoes before the sanctum. Look for designated shoe stalls or carry a bag to keep them with you. Wash feet and hands if a water tap is provided near the entrance.

## Photography rules

Assume photography is forbidden inside the inner sanctum unless you see explicit signs permitting it. Flash is almost always inappropriate. Drone photography over temple complexes is restricted in many states.

### Special ceremonies

During aarti (lamp ceremonies), remain still and quiet. Don't walk in front of people praying. If offered prasad (blessed food), accept it with your right hand.

## Women visitors

Some temples restrict entry during menstruation or require sarongs for women in pants. Research specific temple policies — Sabarimala and certain Shakti peeths have unique rules.

## Gifts and donations

Donation boxes support temple maintenance. Haggling is inappropriate. If a priest offers a blessing, a small donation is customary but never mandatory.
