Goan food is a love letter to the sea, Portugal, and Konkan spice markets. Skip the generic tourist menus and follow this trail through markets, bakeries, and roadside stalls.

## Morning: Panjim and Fontainhas

Start at **Hotel Venite** for pork vindaloo and poi bread in a heritage courtyard. Walk the Latin Quarter, then grab a **cutlet pao** from a street cart near Municipal Market.

## Midday: Mapusa Friday Market

Friday is essential. Sample **kokum sherbet** to beat the heat, buy fresh **recheado masala** fish, and try **ros omelette** — a local street invention of eggs topped with spicy gravy.

### Afternoon snack

**Sorpatel** at a local home-kitchen (ask your guesthouse for recommendations) pairs beautifully with feni — Goa's cashew spirit — if you're comfortable with strong drinks.

## Evening: North Goa beaches

Beach shacks excel at **prawn balchão** and **crab xec xec**. Look for places where locals eat, not only sunset-view tables. Agree on price per kilo for seafood before it's cooked.

## Dessert stop

**Bebinca** — layered coconut cake — is best from traditional bakeries in Saligao or Margao. Buy a full wheel to share; it improves overnight.

## Food safety tips

Eat where turnover is high, avoid raw salads from unknown stalls, and carry hand sanitiser. Your stomach will thank you on day two of the trail.
