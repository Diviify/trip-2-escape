The monsoon transforms India into a lush, mist-draped wonderland — but it demands a different travel mindset. With the right planning, June through September can be your favourite season.

## Understanding the monsoon map

India doesn't rain uniformly. The Western Ghats (Lonavala, Mahabaleshwar, Goa) receive heavy coastal showers, while Rajasthan sees brief, dramatic storms. The Himalayas get intermittent rain with clearer mornings.

## Best monsoon destinations

### Western Ghats
Waterfalls swell to their fullest. Book hill-station stays with valley views and carry quick-dry clothing.

### Kerala backwaters
Houseboats glide through emerald channels. Mosquito repellent is essential after dusk.

### Spiti and Ladakh
The rain shadow keeps these regions relatively dry — ideal for photographers who want mood without constant downpours.

## Packing for wet weather

Quick-dry fabrics, a packable rain jacket, waterproof phone pouch, and silica gel packets for camera gear are non-negotiable. Pack an extra pair of shoes — wet feet ruin even the best itinerary.

## Transport and safety

Mountain roads can close during landslides. Build buffer days into Himalayan trips and check regional weather alerts daily. Trains are often more reliable than hill-road drives during peak rain.

## Silver linings

Fewer crowds, lower hotel rates, and landscapes that look nothing like the dry season make monsoon travel deeply rewarding for flexible travellers.
