Rishikesh is India's rafting capital. The Ganges roars through boulder gardens and calm pools against a backdrop of ashrams and forested cliffs — accessible to first-timers yet thrilling for experts.

## Understanding rapid grades

- **Grade I–II** — Gentle flows, ideal for families
- **Grade III** — The classic 16 km "Marine Drive" stretch; exciting but manageable
- **Grade IV+** — Seasonal, for experienced rafters only (typically post-monsoon)

## Best season

September to June offers reliable flows. Monsoon (July–August) often suspends operations due to dangerous water levels. Spring water is cold — wetsuits are provided by reputable operators.

## Choosing an operator

Verify:

- Government-licensed guides with first-aid certification
- Helmet and life jacket in good condition
- Clear briefing on swimming position and rescue signals
- Insurance coverage included in the package price

### The classic itinerary

Morning pickup from your hotel, shuttle to Shivpuri or Marine Drive put-in, 2–3 hours on the river with a beach stop for cliff jumping (optional), and lunch on the banks before return.

## Safety essentials

Never raft intoxicated. Secure glasses with straps. Leave electronics in dry bags or at the hotel. Listen to your guide — they've read this river thousands of times.

## Combine with yoga

Balance adrenaline with a sunrise yoga session. Many ashrams and boutique retreats package rafting with wellness programmes for the ultimate Rishikesh weekend.
