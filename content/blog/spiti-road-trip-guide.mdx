Spiti is not a casual weekend getaway. At 4,000+ metres, this high-altitude desert demands respect, preparation, and at least seven to ten days on the road.

## Route options

### Manali–Kaza (classic)
Cross Rohtang Pass and Kunzum Pass. Open roughly June to October. Stunning but demanding — hire a local driver experienced in mountain conditions.

### Shimla–Kaza (gradual acclimatisation)
Longer but gentler altitude gain. Recommended for first-time high-altitude travellers.

## Permits and paperwork

Indian nationals need ID; foreign nationals require Inner Line Permits for certain areas. Carry photocopies and digital backups. Check current restrictions for Rohtang — vehicle quotas apply in peak season.

## Acclimatisation schedule

Never rush altitude. Spend a night in Manali, then Kaza before pushing to Chandratal or villages above 4,200 m. Watch for headache, nausea, and dizziness — descend if symptoms worsen.

### Must-see stops

- **Key Monastery** — thousand-year-old Buddhist seat overlooking the Spiti River
- **Chandratal Lake** — crescent moon lake; camp under the stars
- **Langza** — fossil village with a giant Buddha overlooking the valley

## Vehicle and fuel

4x4 is strongly recommended. Fuel stations are sparse — top up at Kaza. Carry spare tyre, toolkit, and cash for emergencies.

## Connectivity

BSNL works intermittently. Download offline maps. Inform family of expected blackout periods — that's part of Spiti's charm.

## When things go wrong

Road closures from landslides happen. Build two buffer days and travel with patience. The valley rewards those who slow down.
