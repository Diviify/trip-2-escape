Rajasthan doesn't just preserve tradition — it stages it. Aligning your visit with a major festival adds layers of colour, music, and meaning to every palace and desert camp.

## Pushkar Camel Fair (October–November)

Thousands of camels, folk musicians, and pilgrims converge on this sacred lake town. Book accommodation six months ahead — tents and heritage havelis fill fast.

## Jaipur Literature Festival (January)

The world's largest free literary festival transforms the Pink City into a global salon of ideas. Even non-readers love the atmosphere in Diggi Palace gardens.

## Desert Festival, Jaisalmer (February)

Three days of turban-tying competitions, camel races, and fire dancers on the Sam Sand Dunes. Pair it with a luxury desert camp stay.

## Holi in Udaipur (March)

The Mewar royal family hosts a ceremonial celebration at the City Palace before the streets explode with colour. Respect local customs and dress in clothes you won't miss.

## Planning tips

Festival dates shift with lunar calendars. Confirm exact dates a year in advance, expect premium pricing, and hire local guides who understand crowd flow and photography etiquette.
